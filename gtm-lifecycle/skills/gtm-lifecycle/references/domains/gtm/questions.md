# GTM — Question Battery

Expert probes for GTM spars — the questions that find where a GTM brief is soft. (The Phase 1 *elicitation* battery lives in `../../question-battery.md`; these are the adversary's questions, run during recon and assault.)

## Who literally pays
"Name the budget line, the signer, and the renewal champion — three different people in most orgs. If you can only name the user, you have an adoption plan, not a GTM."
date: 2026-07-06 · source: buying-group research (Gartner B2B buying journey); RethinkDB/Docker case record in failure-modes.md · grade: B

## The real alternative
"If you didn't exist, what would the customer actually do — which incumbent, spreadsheet, intern, or nothing-at-all? Position against THAT, not against the competitor list in your head."
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Dunford, Obviously Awesome, 2019 (competitive-alternatives step) · grade: B

## What gets fired
"When they hire you, what do they fire — a tool, a process, a person's Tuesday? What does the firing cost them in switching pain and politics? No firing = you're an addition, and additions get consolidated."
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Christensen et al., JTBD, HBR 2016 · grade: A

## The wedge sharpness test
"For ONE user with ONE job, where do you clearly beat their current alternative — not impress, BEAT? If the answer lists three users or four jobs, there's no wedge yet."
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Moore, Crossing the Chasm, 1991/2014 (beachhead discipline) · grade: B

## Motion-ACV arithmetic
"Multiply your price by realistic volume: does the ACV pay for the motion that sells it? A $99/mo product cannot fund an AE; a $100k platform cannot be bought self-serve. Which of Janz's animals are you hunting, and with what weapon?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Janz, "Five ways to build a $100M business," Point Nine, 2014 · grade: B

## The next ten customers
"Name the next 10 customers who look IDENTICAL to each other. If each sale needs a different story, you have ten markets of one — repeatability, not revenue, is what the first sales must prove."
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Moore beachhead discipline; Roberge, The Sales Acceleration Formula, 2015 (repeatability before scaling) · grade: B

## Demo-to-dollar gap
"Everything between 'they loved the demo' and money hitting the account: security review, procurement, legal, integration, the champion changing jobs. How long is that pipe for YOUR buyer, and does it fit inside your runway?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: enterprise-sales practitioner canon; pilot-purgatory base rate (McKinsey 2017–2020, base-rates.md) · grade: B

## Dogfood generalization probe
"You built it for yourselves — which parts of the internal job are actually YOUR idiosyncrasies (your stack, your team shape, your tolerance for rough edges)? What breaks the first time an org that isn't you tries to run it?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: dogfood-to-product failure pattern (RethinkDB post-mortem, defmacro.org 2017; general platform-extraction practitioner canon) · grade: B

## Category cost question
"Existing category, subcategory, or new category — and can you AFFORD the one you picked? Existing = comparison you must win; new = a market-education bill only the well-funded survive. Who pays for the buyer learning what you are?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Dunford, Obviously Awesome, 2019; category-creation caution entry in base-rates.md · grade: B

## The pricing anchor door
"Your first ten customers anchor on this pricing model forever — migrations churn them. Which value metric scales with the value THEY receive, and are you sure enough to walk through a one-way door on it?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Price Intelligently/Paddle pricing research; Poyar usage-based pricing playbooks (OpenView/Growth Unhinged) · grade: B

## The rep-free journey
"Buyers spend ~17% of the journey with any supplier and mostly prefer no rep at all. Walk me through your buyer's journey with NO human from your side in it — what do they read, try, and compare? If the answer is 'nothing yet,' that's the GTM backlog."
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Gartner B2B buying-journey research + 2025/2026 rep-free surveys (gartner.com) · grade: A

## Revenue-ranked incumbent sweep (recon standing rule)
"Name the LARGEST company already monetizing this exact buyer — by revenue/users, not the most similar-looking startup." Run at recon, always: a GoHighLevel-class miss (the giant hiding in the very listicle you cited) is disqualifying — rerun the sweep.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: GTM recon practice · grade: B

## Deflation
"Is the value you sell deflating — AI/commoditization shrinking it quarterly? Which half of the value prop deflates slower — sell that half."
date: 2026-07-06 · source: GTM strategy practice · grade: B

## Null hypothesis
"What is the company already doing that this strategy competes with for marginal hours? The default motion enters the ranking as the null hypothesis — every other motion must state why a marginal hour beats it."
date: 2026-07-06 · source: GTM strategy practice · grade: B

## Evidence velocity
"What is the fastest instrument producing an unambiguous verdict on the core unknown — cost in days and dollars? Payment-grade evidence preferred: payment > usage > signup > attention."
date: 2026-07-06 · source: GTM strategy practice · grade: B
