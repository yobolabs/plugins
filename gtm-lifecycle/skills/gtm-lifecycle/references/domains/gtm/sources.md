# GTM — Sources

## Obviously Awesome + Sales Pitch (Dunford, 2019 / 2023)
The positioning methodology (competitive alternatives → attributes → value → segment → category) and its sales-narrative companion. The sharpest working tools for positioning spars.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: April Dunford, Obviously Awesome (2019), Sales Pitch (2023); aprildunford.com · grade: B

## Crossing the Chasm + Inside the Tornado (Moore, 1991/2014, 1995)
Beachhead/wedge discipline, pragmatist reference dynamics, bowling-pin sequencing. The canonical GTM sequencing texts.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Geoffrey Moore, HarperBusiness · grade: A (canonical, decades of validation)

## Product-Led Growth (Bush, 2019) + the OpenView PLG corpus
PLG motion selection, free-model choice (freemium vs trial), time-to-value framing. OpenView wound down Dec 2023 — archive still authoritative; the living successor practice is Kyle Poyar's Growth Unhinged.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Wes Bush, Product-Led Growth, 2019; openviewpartners.com archive; growthunhinged.com · grade: B

## Lenny's Newsletter benchmark series (Rachitsky × Poyar)
Free-to-paid conversion, activation, retention, growth-model benchmarks from 1,000+ product surveys — the working reference class for consumer/prosumer/B2B-SaaS funnel verdicts.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: lennysnewsletter.com/t/benchmarks (esp. "What is a good free-to-paid conversion rate?") · grade: B

## The Sales Acceleration Formula (Roberge, 2015)
Founder-led → repeatable → scaled sales sequencing, with HubSpot's actual staged record. The antidote to premature sales hires.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Mark Roberge, Wiley, 2015 · grade: B

## Point Nine / Christoph Janz
"Five ways to build a $100M business" (flies→elephants ACV-motion arithmetic) and the SaaS Funding Napkin series — motion-vs-price fit checks.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: christophjanz.blogspot.com; pointnine.capital · grade: B

## Gartner B2B buying research
Buying-journey time allocation (17% with suppliers, 5–6% per rep), buying-group composition, rep-free preference surveys (61% 2025 → 67% 2026). Primary data on how buyers actually buy.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: gartner.com/en/sales/insights/b2b-buying-journey + Gartner newsroom sales surveys · grade: A

## Annual private-SaaS surveys: KeyBanc/Sapphire-style + RepVue + Bridge Group
Yearly medians for churn/NRR/CAC-payback (KeyBanc-lineage surveys), quota attainment (RepVue Cloud Sales Index, Bridge Group AE reports). Pull the CURRENT year at mission time — these numbers drift; that's the point of them.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: sapphireventures.com / key.com research; repvue.com; bridgegroupinc.com · grade: B

## Tomasz Tunguz
SMB/mid-market/enterprise churn bands, SaaS benchmarking analyses, pricing/motion economics. Practitioner-quant; pair with survey primaries for load-bearing numbers.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: tomtunguz.com · grade: B

## CB Insights startup post-mortem series
GTM-relevant failure causes ("no market need," unit economics) across hundreds of shutdowns. Vendor research, methodology opaque — directional.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: cbinsights.com/research/report/startup-failure-reasons-top · grade: B

## First Round Review + SaaStr
Deep practitioner case interviews on first sales hires, pricing changes, positioning rollouts. Anecdote-rich — mine for failure signals and countermoves, not base rates.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: review.firstround.com; saastr.com · grade: B

## Primary case record: founder post-mortems + SEC filings + investigative press
RethinkDB (defmacro.org, 2017), Everpix (The Verge, 2013), Homejoy (Backchannel, 2015), Docker/Mirantis (2019), Zenefits (SEC 2017), MoviePass (HMNY filings, FTC 2021). Always preferred over retellings when a case is load-bearing.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: defmacro.org; theverge.com; wired.com; sec.gov/edgar; ftc.gov · grade: A
