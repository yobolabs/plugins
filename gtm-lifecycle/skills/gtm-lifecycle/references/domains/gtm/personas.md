# GTM — Personas

The adversaries a GTM brief must survive. Four persona fields each (stance / incentives / attacks-first / signature question). Complements the `business-strategy` cast (Incumbent CEO, Burned Operator, Unit-Economics Auditor, Distribution Cynic).

## The Skeptical Investor
- stance: has seen this deck category forty times this year; assumes the wedge is a feature, the TAM slide is top-down, and "we'll figure out pricing" means nobody wants to hear the answer.
- incentives: protect the fund from the base rates; back only plans whose bets are cheap to test and whose motion fits the team that will run it.
- attacks first: why-now (what changed?); wedge sharpness (one user, one job, or a grab-bag?); the motion-vs-team mismatch (a sales-led plan from a team with no sellers); which falsifiable bet dies first and what it costs to find out.
- signature question: "What do you know about this buyer that the market doesn't — and what's the cheapest observation that would prove you wrong?"
- date: 2026-07-06 · source: VC diligence practice canon (Sequoia pitch template's why-now; Gurley TAM critiques); base-rates.md conversion/attainment entries · grade: B

## The Category Incumbent
- stance: owns the category vocabulary, the analyst relationships, and the customer's renewal calendar; your differentiator reads to them as next quarter's feature announcement.
- incentives: keep buyers framing purchases in the category they define; absorb genuine threats as checklist features before the market notices the difference.
- attacks first: your differentiator's copyability ("a weekend for my platform team"); your category choice (inside their category you're a line-item comparison they win; creating your own, you pay the education bill); your access to THEIR install base's switching moments.
- signature question: "Why doesn't this become a feature of my product next quarter — and when my marketing budget outspends your existence, what does your positioning weigh?"
- date: 2026-07-06 · source: modeled on counter-positioning / feature-absorption dynamics (Helmer, 7 Powers, 2016; Dunford, Obviously Awesome, 2019 on category framing) · grade: B

## The CFO Buyer
- stance: doesn't attend the demo; meets the product for the first time at renewal, as a line item on a consolidation list. Sentiment-proof.
- incentives: cut tool sprawl; fund only line items with an owner, an attributed number, and a screamer if cancelled.
- attacks first: which budget line this comes out of (new budget = hardest sale); ROI attribution ("the team likes it" is not a number); seat-count honesty at renewal; what existing spend it replaces (replacement sells; addition gets consolidated).
- signature question: "Whose number does this make bigger, which line does it come out of, and who screams — with data — if I cancel it in Q4?"
- date: 2026-07-06 · source: buying-group/renewal dynamics per Gartner B2B buying research (gartner.com/en/sales/insights/b2b-buying-journey); SaaS consolidation-era practitioner canon · grade: B

## The Burned Founder
- stance: ran this exact positioning last cycle — the platform play, the dev-love wedge, the dual-track — and has the cap table scars; allergic to "our dogfood generalizes".
- incentives: stop history rhyming; reputation as the one who asked the question everyone thanked later.
- attacks first: dogfood-generalization (which parts of the internal job are your OWN idiosyncrasies?); platform-before-app sequencing ("nobody bought my platform before the app proved it either"); the demo-to-dollar gap (security review, procurement, champion turnover); dual-track survivability at your headcount.
- signature question: "I ran this play in [year] — the demo sold, the contract didn't. What's structurally different about yours, specifically, not vibes?"
- date: 2026-07-06 · source: persona standard in VC/founder diligence; grounded in failure-modes.md cases (RethinkDB, Docker, Fab.com) · grade: B

## The Overloaded Champion
- stance: your fan inside the buyer org — and the person who must sell you internally to security, procurement, finance, and their own boss, on top of their day job.
- incentives: look smart for sponsoring wins; never again sponsor a tool that embarrassed them; spend political capital only where the internal sale is winnable.
- attacks first: the internal-sale toolkit you didn't ship (one-pager for their boss, security answers, pricing that survives procurement); onboarding lift on THEIR calendar; what they say when it breaks; whether your pricing forces a signature level above their sponsor.
- signature question: "I get 30 minutes of political capital a month. Walk me through the meeting where I spend it on you — who's in the room and what do I show them?"
- date: 2026-07-06 · source: Gartner buying-group research (multi-stakeholder B2B purchases); champion-enablement practitioner canon (challenger/champion sales literature) · grade: B

## Design-Partner Veteran (0→1 platform operator)
- stance: has personally recruited paid design partners for zero-signal platforms; treats "we cannot sell" as almost always a story, not a fact.
- incentives: evidence velocity + payment-grade proof; allergic to instruments that defer the one conversation that matters.
- attacks first: constraint inflation ("no sales force" hiding "no selling"); passive detectors where the signal must be manufactured; free pilots that measure politeness not demand; strategies that wait for inbound that must be created.
- signature question: "Who did you personally pitch this week — and did you ask them to pay? If the answer is nobody, your constraint is an excuse, not a limit."
- date: 2026-07-06 · source: standard 0→1 platform founder-led design-partner motion; this calibration (constraint-inflation case) · grade: B
