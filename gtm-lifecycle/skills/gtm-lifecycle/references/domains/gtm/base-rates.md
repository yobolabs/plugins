# GTM — Base Rates

The numbers that call bullshit on GTM plans. For survival/M&A/pioneer base rates, load the `business-strategy` pack alongside.

## Free-to-paid conversion (reference class: 1,000+ SaaS products, 2023 survey)
Freemium self-serve: 3–5% is GOOD, 6–8% GREAT. Freemium + sales-assist: 5–7% good, 10–15% great. Opt-in free trial: 8–12% good, 15–25% great (credit-card-required trials convert higher still, but sign up ~5% vs freemium's ~9%). Any plan modeling >10% freemium conversion without a mechanism is fantasy.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Rachitsky & Poyar benchmark survey, Lenny's Newsletter, "What is a good free-to-paid conversion rate?" (lennysnewsletter.com/p/what-is-a-good-free-to-paid-conversion) · grade: B (large practitioner survey, self-reported)

## Sales quota attainment ≈ half
43–57% of B2B reps hit quota across studies; Bridge Group 2024: 51% average; RepVue Q4 2024 Cloud Sales Index: ~43%. Top-quartile orgs reach 65–75%. A revenue model assuming every hired rep delivers full quota is off by ~2× on day one.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Bridge Group SaaS AE reports; RepVue Cloud Sales Index Q4 2024 (repvue.com); CSO Insights/Salesforce State of Sales (convergent 45–50%) · grade: B (convergent multi-survey)

## Buyers spend ~17% of the journey with suppliers
B2B buying groups spend ~17% of the purchase journey meeting potential suppliers — 5–6% with any single vendor's reps; 61–67% prefer rep-free buying (Gartner surveys 2025–2026). Content/docs/pricing-page carry most of the funnel whether the plan admits it or not.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Gartner B2B buying-journey research + press releases 2025-06-25, 2026-03-09 (gartner.com) · grade: A

## Cold outreach response rate
Only ~8.5% of cold outreach emails get ANY response (12.3M-email study); booked meetings are a low single-digit fraction of sends. Multi-touch and personalization improve it; nothing makes cold email a primary motion for a two-person team without dedicated capacity.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Backlinko × Pitchbox outreach study, 2019 (backlinko.com/email-outreach-study) · grade: B

## Enterprise pilot purgatory
84% of companies running IoT/digital pilots were stuck in pilot mode >1 year; 28% >2 years (McKinsey survey, May 2017; later McKinsey manufacturing surveys 2019–2020 found 56–74% stuck). Unpaid or success-criteria-free enterprise pilots are where GTM motions go to die — price the pilot, pre-agree conversion terms.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: McKinsey "escaping pilot purgatory" digital-manufacturing research, 2017–2020 (mckinsey.com) · grade: B

## T2D3 is top-decile, not median
The celebrated SaaS growth path — triple, triple, double, double, double from ~$2M ARR to $144M in 5 years — describes breakout companies (the pattern behind many SaaS IPOs), not the median startup. Using T2D3 as a plan is planning to be top-decile; fine as ambition, dishonest as a base case.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Agrawal (Battery Ventures), "The SaaS Adventure," TechCrunch, Feb 2015 · grade: B

## Churn scales inversely with customer size
Practitioner-convergent bands: SMB customers churn ~3–7%/month; mid-market ~1–2%/month; enterprise ~0.5–1%/month. SMB-motion plans must model replacing a third or more of the base annually — LTV math built on enterprise-grade retention with SMB customers is the classic spreadsheet lie.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Tunguz (tomtunguz.com) SMB churn analyses; convergent with ProfitWell/ChartMogul benchmark publications · grade: B (practitioner convergence, bands not point estimates)

## "No market need" kills more than competition
Poor product-market fit appears in ~43% of VC-backed shutdown post-mortems (2023–2025 sample); earlier rounds: "no market need" 35–42%. Competitors appear far less often. The wedge question ("who fires what to hire you?") is the highest-leverage de-risking question in the battery.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: CB Insights startup-failure post-mortem series, 2021 + 2025 editions · grade: B

## Category-creation market-cap claim — CAUTION entry
The widely quoted "category kings capture 76% of the category's market cap" comes from Play Bigger (2016) with undisclosed methodology; no peer-reviewed replication found. Treat it as marketing for category creation, not evidence. Pair with the cost side: category creation requires educating a market — a spend profile most seed-stage teams cannot survive.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Ramadan et al., Play Bigger, 2016 (claim origin); absence-of-replication note after literature search 2026-07-06 · grade: C (deliberate caution entry)

## Retention/NRR benchmarks drift — re-verify at use
Public-SaaS NRR medians moved from ~115–120% (2021 peak) toward ~105–110% (2024) as expansion slowed; private-company survey medians (KeyBanc/Sapphire-style) move year to year. Do not cite an NRR benchmark from memory in a verdict — pull the current year's survey. This entry exists to force the hunt.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: directional from Meritech public-SaaS comps + annual KeyBanc/Sapphire private SaaS surveys; point values deliberately omitted · grade: C (deliberate re-verify tripwire)

## Time to $1M ARR
~2.75 years median for SaaS startups reaching $1M ARR. Assuming materially faster is planning to be top-decile — fine as ambition, dishonest as a base case.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: 2026 SaaS benchmarks · grade: B

## Agency-tool price points
AI/automation platforms sold to agencies and SMB operators cluster at $300–1,500/mo (GoHighLevel-class chassis pricing). Price a competing motion against this band, not against enterprise ACVs.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: 2026 agency-platform public pricing · grade: B

## Founder-led design-partner sales = the standard 0→1 platform motion
The default first motion for a 0→1 platform: founder-led sales to 3–5 paid design partners. Founder-led selling to N≤5 is not a sales force; a plan that forbids it under a no-sales-force constraint has inflated the constraint.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: 0→1 platform practitioner canon · grade: B
