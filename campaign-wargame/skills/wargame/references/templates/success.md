# War-Game Acceptance Checklist

A campaign war-game becomes the launch runbook only when ALL of these hold:

1. **Runnable cold** — someone who wasn't in the room could execute it: names, counts, links, thresholds all literal.
2. **Every move complete** — both expectations (works / doesn't), most-likely failure with early-warning signal, both responses. Can't-take-it-back moves have pre-flight checks.
3. **Something fails on paper** — a war-game where every move succeeds is a wish; rejected outright.
4. **Facts are honest** — every important claim tagged; "checked" facts have named sources; "don't knows" were hunted, survivors sit in open questions with an owner.
5. **Expensive ripples walked** — list health, discount training, channel rating, promise stacking: numbered chains, honest stopping points.
6. **Upside examined** — opportunities ranked; every "later" has a trigger, every "set-up-for" a hook.
7. **One habit challenged** — at least one "that's how we do it" got a why, and either a verified reason or a small test.
8. **Brakes installed** — watch-points with pause thresholds AND explicit stop conditions. No brakes, no launch.
9. **Simple enough** — the campaign itself passes the Better rubric; if a simpler route to the same goal exists, the war-game says so.
10. **After-action section exists and WILL be filled** — the library only grows from real campaigns.
