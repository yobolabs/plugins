# Traps: Hierarchy Failures

Layout failures where the ORDER of perception breaks - the viewer's one glance is spent on the wrong thing, on everything, or on nothing. This class is the failure shadow of the visual-hierarchy pack: every entry below is a violated VH directive wearing its most common costume. These defects are mostly born at layout/composite stage rather than inside the generator, so several negative-prompt fields double primarily as reviewer-checklist phrasing; they still steer generation toward compositions that leave the hierarchy buildable.

Entries carry the eight schema fields; craft-file avoid-lists cite ids; the critic and execute's L2 vision check use the markers, always at actual render size - hierarchy verdicts made on a zoomed canvas are void.

### hier-everything-shouts
- id: hier-everything-shouts
- anti-pattern: Every element at maximum emphasis - big, bold, bright, badged, all competing at once.
- markers: Three or more elements at similar visual weight; the squint test produces no first read; multiple starbursts, badges, or "NEW" flashes; every text line styled as a headline; no quiet zone anywhere in frame.
- why-it-fails: Emphasis is relational and budgeted (visual-hierarchy VH-2, VH-5); when everything shouts, the viewer's triage returns nothing and the scroll continues - loudness that cancels itself.
- negative-prompt: cluttered layout, busy composition, overcrowded design, competing elements, every element bold, badge overload
- severity: kills-the-asset
- provenance-class: published craft knowledge
- privacy-check: pass - generalized layout-failure class; no merchant, campaign, or customer data

### hier-buried-cta
- id: hier-buried-cta
- anti-pattern: The action visually last or absent - a CTA that is small, low-contrast, off the eye path, or missing because "the platform adds one" when it does not.
- markers: The squint test loses the CTA entirely; CTA color blends into its background region; CTA placed in a corner the engineered eye path never crosses; direct-response asset with no action anywhere.
- why-it-fails: Direct response dies without a findable action (conversion-craft CV-4, visual-hierarchy VH-6); an ad that stops the thumb and then offers nowhere to go converts attention into nothing.
- negative-prompt: hidden call to action, low contrast button, tiny button text, missing call to action area
- severity: kills-the-asset
- provenance-class: published craft knowledge
- privacy-check: pass - generalized layout-failure class; no merchant, campaign, or customer data

### hier-focal-conflict
- id: hier-focal-conflict
- anti-pattern: Two or more elements fighting to be the focal point - the frame has candidates instead of a winner.
- markers: The eye bounces between two regions on first viewing; a face and a product at equal weight looking in different directions; two high-contrast zones at opposite corners; stakeholder additions ("make the logo bigger too") that promoted a second winner.
- why-it-fails: Hierarchy exists to make the viewing decision FOR the viewer (visual-hierarchy VH-1); a conflict hands the decision back, and the viewer resolves it by leaving.
- negative-prompt: two subjects competing for attention, split focus composition, dual focal points, subject and object equally prominent
- severity: hurts
- provenance-class: published craft knowledge
- privacy-check: pass - generalized layout-failure class; no merchant, campaign, or customer data

### hier-logo-first
- id: hier-logo-first
- anti-pattern: The vanity layout - logo dominant, message subordinate; the brand crowned where the hook should be.
- markers: Logo is the largest element or the first read under squint; brand name repeated in multiple places; message and offer crowded to clear room for the mark; the exit position (where the CTA belongs) occupied by branding.
- why-it-fails: Viewers do not stop scrolling for logos; the brand is the SIGNATURE of the message, confirmed at the exit, not the message itself (visual-hierarchy VH-1, VH-6). Leading with the mark is a strategy available to icon brands as a deliberate call - as a default it is vanity spending the hook.
- negative-prompt: oversized logo, logo dominating composition, watermark branding across image, repeated brand marks
- severity: hurts
- provenance-class: published craft knowledge
- privacy-check: pass - generalized layout-failure class; no merchant, campaign, or customer data

### hier-text-wall
- id: hier-text-wall
- anti-pattern: Paragraph-length copy at feed scale - the asset demands reading where the medium only grants a glance.
- markers: Three or more lines of body text on a feed asset; type at sizes illegible at render scale; full sentences where labels belong; the message unreadable without zooming.
- why-it-fails: Feed assets are triaged, not read (visual-hierarchy's first principle, typography TY-1/TY-3); a wall of text signals effort demanded, and the viewer declines the transaction before knowing what it was.
- negative-prompt: dense paragraph text, wall of text, tiny unreadable text, cluttered text overlay, long body copy on image
- severity: kills-the-asset
- provenance-class: session corpus
- privacy-check: pass - generalized layout-failure class; no merchant, campaign, or customer data

### hier-no-focal
- id: hier-no-focal
- anti-pattern: The wallpaper effect - no element wins at all; an even texture of mild elements the eye slides across.
- markers: Squint yields uniform mush with no first read; nothing recallable after one glance; all elements between 60 and 80 percent emphasis with nothing at 100; the asset reads as background pattern next to organic content.
- why-it-fails: An ad with no first read is processed as ambient noise and skipped without registering (visual-hierarchy VH-1, VH-7) - the mirror-image failure of everything-shouts, arriving at the same zero.
- negative-prompt: flat uniform composition, no clear subject, wallpaper pattern layout, evenly weighted elements, monotonous composition
- severity: hurts
- provenance-class: published craft knowledge
- privacy-check: pass - generalized layout-failure class; no merchant, campaign, or customer data

### hier-corner-scatter
- id: hier-corner-scatter
- anti-pattern: Elements pinned to corners and edges with no grouping logic - logo one corner, CTA the opposite, headline floating free; the eye has no path, only stops.
- markers: No proximity clusters; whitespace trapped in the center while content hugs the frame; related items (price and product, claim and qualifier) separated; the eye path sketch (1st/2nd/3rd) cannot be drawn without doubling back.
- why-it-fails: Proximity is meaning (visual-hierarchy VH-4); scattered elements are read as unrelated fragments, so the viewer receives four notices instead of one message.
- negative-prompt: scattered layout elements, disconnected composition, elements floating in corners, no visual grouping
- severity: hurts
- provenance-class: published craft knowledge
- privacy-check: pass - generalized layout-failure class; no merchant, campaign, or customer data
