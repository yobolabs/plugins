# Traps: Cliche & Inauthentic

Contrived and cliche patterns audiences reject - and increasingly mock in public. This is the flagship trap class (D5): the render can be technically flawless and the ad still reads FAKE, because the failure is in the scenario, the casting, the emotion, or the borrowed grammar, not the pixels. The theory of WHY these fail lives in the authenticity pack; this file is the pattern library.

Entries are generalized by audience class - never a named merchant, brand, or campaign incident. What any specific audience is currently mocking (live examples, fresh cliches, expired trends) is perishable knowledge: recon's authenticity hunt fetches it per mission and the craft file carries it as mission-specific guardrails; this file carries only the durable pattern classes. Each entry carries the eight schema fields; craft-file avoid-lists cite ids, execute feeds negative-prompt phrasing verbatim, and the critic uses markers as its authenticity red-team checklist.

### cliche-stock-energy
- id: cliche-stock-energy
- anti-pattern: The stock-photo scenario - posed models performing product enjoyment nobody performs: laughing alone at a salad, boardroom high-fives, pointing at a whiteboard while grinning.
- markers: Emotion intensity exceeds the product's stakes; every subject camera-aware; wardrobe and grooming uniform across the group; gesture props (raised mugs, pointing fingers) with no plausible cause; the moment cannot be narrated ("why are they laughing?").
- why-it-fails: Scenarios that exist only in advertising are the fake-detector's primary training data (authenticity AU-1); audiences have named and memed the entire genre, so the asset inherits the mockery of its class.
- negative-prompt: stock photo style, posed models, staged scene, cheesy smile, forced laughter, people pointing at screens, generic business people, camera-aware posing
- severity: kills-the-asset
- provenance-class: expert-conversation notes
- privacy-check: pass - generalized pattern class; no merchant, campaign, or customer data

### cliche-overpolish
- id: cliche-overpolish
- anti-pattern: Everything perfect - spotless kitchens, ironed lives, symmetric styling, zero texture of actual use anywhere in frame.
- markers: No wear, crumbs, cables, fingerprints, or asymmetry in a supposedly lived-in scene; every surface showroom-grade; food styled past edibility; a home with no evidence of inhabitants.
- why-it-fails: Perfection is the signature of staging; audiences trust texture and evidence of life (authenticity AU-2). Family and value-shopper audiences are especially tuned to domestic implausibility - a kitchen they could never have reads as an ad for someone else.
- negative-prompt: overly polished, showroom perfect, sterile environment, pristine staged interior, artificial perfection, immaculate untouched surfaces
- severity: hurts
- provenance-class: published craft knowledge
- privacy-check: pass - generalized pattern class; no merchant, campaign, or customer data

### cliche-diversity-tableau
- id: cliche-diversity-tableau
- anti-pattern: Engineered one-of-each casting arranged like a brochure - representation as a checkbox grid rather than a plausible group of people who know each other.
- markers: Group composition reads as exactly one person per demographic; identical styling and identical smiles across the group; everyone facing the camera; no visible social relationship or shared activity that would bring this group together.
- why-it-fails: Audiences - most of all the groups being represented - read checkbox casting as performative box-ticking; a specific plausible group (whoever it contains) reads as real, while an engineered tableau reads as the brand talking about itself.
- negative-prompt: brochure group photo, staged diverse group facing camera, corporate group stock photo, artificial group arrangement, everyone smiling at camera
- severity: hurts
- provenance-class: expert-conversation notes
- privacy-check: pass - generalized pattern class; no merchant, campaign, or customer data

### cliche-corporate-smile
- id: cliche-corporate-smile
- anti-pattern: Forced-optimism corporate theater - handshakes as hero images, suited grins at laptops, thumbs-up at the camera, triumphant conference rooms.
- markers: Emotion unlinked to any depicted cause; a handshake or high-five as the focal point; uniform smile intensity across every face; office scenes containing no actual work.
- why-it-fails: Professional audiences see their real work life nowhere in it; the gap between depicted euphoria and actual office reality reads as a company that does not know its customers (authenticity AU-4 - emotion past the plausible intensity is a lie about the product).
- negative-prompt: business handshake, thumbs up pose, corporate stock smile, suited executives grinning, staged office celebration, triumphant boardroom
- severity: hurts
- provenance-class: expert-conversation notes
- privacy-check: pass - generalized pattern class; no merchant, campaign, or customer data

### cliche-slop-aesthetic
- id: cliche-slop-aesthetic
- anti-pattern: The AI-slop house style - hyper-saturated cinematic gloss, dramatic rim light on everything, bokeh soup, the averaged AI face, ornamental hyper-detail with no meaning.
- markers: Maximum saturation plus maximum contrast plus shallow depth of field on every subject; lighting like a movie trailer regardless of scene; subjects with the interchangeable idealized AI face; intricate detail that decorates nothing; an overall glow no real lens produces.
- why-it-fails: Audiences now recognize the default output style of lazy generation on sight; the style itself has become the tell that no human cared enough to direct it (authenticity AU-6, color-contrast CC-7). It fails hardest with digital-native audiences, who treat it as content pollution.
- negative-prompt: oversaturated colors, hyperdetailed, cinematic lighting, dramatic rim lighting, epic render, glossy 3d render look, airbrushed fantasy style, artstation trending style
- severity: kills-the-asset
- provenance-class: expert-conversation notes
- privacy-check: pass - generalized pattern class; no merchant, campaign, or customer data

### cliche-lifestyle-nobody-lives
- id: cliche-lifestyle-nobody-lives
- anti-pattern: A contrived aspirational scenario disconnected from the product's actual place in life - rooftop champagne for a budget offer, sunrise yoga serenity for back-office software.
- markers: A setting the target audience will never occupy; the product's role in the scene implausible on narration; aspiration borrowed from an unrelated category's playbook; the depicted life contradicting the price point.
- why-it-fails: Aspiration works when it is one believable step up from the viewer's life, not a different life entirely; past that gap the ad reads as the brand lying about who it is for, and the viewer correctly concludes "not me".
- negative-prompt: luxury lifestyle cliche, rooftop champagne scene, generic aspirational setting, unrealistic lifestyle photography, yacht and sunset cliche
- severity: hurts
- provenance-class: published craft knowledge
- privacy-check: pass - generalized pattern class; no merchant, campaign, or customer data

### cliche-youth-pander
- id: cliche-youth-pander
- anti-pattern: Borrowed youth-culture grammar misused - slang, meme formats, and creator mannerisms deployed by a brand with no credible claim to them.
- markers: Trend grammar visibly older than the audience's current cycle; slang inside an otherwise corporate voice; skateboard/neon/graffiti shorthand standing in for "young"; meme structure with the timing wrong. Whether a specific reference is alive or expired is recon's hunt (INV-5) - designed-from-memory trend content should be treated as expired by default.
- why-it-fails: Digital-native audiences are the most sensitive fake-detector class and police cultural borrowing publicly; trying-too-hard is punished harder than being straightforwardly a brand (authenticity AU-8). The failure mode is not being skipped - it is becoming the joke.
- negative-prompt: try-hard youth marketing style, forced meme aesthetic, skateboard stock imagery, neon graffiti backdrop, corporate slang overlay
- severity: kills-the-asset
- provenance-class: expert-conversation notes
- privacy-check: pass - generalized pattern class; no merchant, campaign, or customer data

### cliche-premium-cosplay
- id: cliche-premium-cosplay
- anti-pattern: Luxury visual grammar - marble, gold foil, whispering serif, black minimalism - applied to an offer that is not luxury.
- markers: Category-standard premium props around a discount message; a visual promise the price point contradicts; restraint styling with none of the material honesty premium buyers verify (real texture, real craft cues).
- why-it-fails: Premium buyers detect cosplay through material and restraint cues and read it as counterfeit; value buyers are alienated by a look that says "not for you"; the asset addresses nobody (authenticity AU-7 - detector-profile mismatch on both sides).
- negative-prompt: gold and marble luxury backdrop, generic premium template, black and gold luxury aesthetic, faux luxury styling
- severity: hurts
- provenance-class: published craft knowledge
- privacy-check: pass - generalized pattern class; no merchant, campaign, or customer data

### cliche-generic-localism
- id: cliche-generic-localism
- anti-pattern: Fake community signaling - "your neighborhood favorite" visuals with no actual local specificity to back the claim.
- markers: The scene could be any city; a stock storefront standing in for a real one; local language in the copy slot with nothing nameable in the image (no real place, dish, face, or street texture); community claimed, community absent.
- why-it-fails: Local audiences know their place intimately; claiming specificity while delivering genericity is a direct, checkable lie (authenticity AU-3, conversion-craft CV-6) - the interchangeability test fails at the exact moment the copy claims it should not.
- negative-prompt: generic storefront, anonymous city street, stock community gathering, interchangeable neighborhood scene
- severity: hurts
- provenance-class: published craft knowledge
- privacy-check: pass - generalized pattern class; no merchant, campaign, or customer data
