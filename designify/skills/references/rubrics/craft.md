# Craft Rubric — hierarchy, balance, typography, color/contrast, composition

Scores the design-craft quality of a craft file's directives and (at L3) of real renders. Honest bands, no fake numeric precision — a number would claim a measurement nobody made. **Judge at actual placement size, never zoomed**: feed-scale is where the asset lives, and most craft failures are invisible at 400%.

## Bands

- **gallery** — a senior designer would show this uncredited without flinching; every dimension below at "ships" and at least two demonstrably better than the placement's ambient standard. Rare by design; cite what earns it.
- **ships** — every dimension passes its checks below. The default acceptable band.
- **needs-work** — one or more dimensions fail; each failure named with its observable and its fix. Bounded: the list must be closable in one director pass.
- **rejected** — failures compound across dimensions, or a single failure kills legibility of the offer. Name the shortest path back (usually: return to the directive, not patch the render).

## Dimensions

### Visual hierarchy
- **Ships:** the eye lands 1st / 2nd / 3rd exactly where the craft file's directive says, tested at placement size; one focal point; contrast (size, weight, position, color) is spent deliberately on that order.
- **Fails:** everything shouts (uniform emphasis = no emphasis); two elements fight for first; the CTA is discovered rather than seen; hierarchy exists in the layers but dies at thumbnail.

### Balance & composition
- **Ships:** intentional structure — grid held or broken on purpose; whitespace doing work (grouping, breathing, pointing); crops confident; nothing floats unanchored.
- **Fails:** trapped whitespace, edge tension nobody chose, center-of-mass drift, elements aligned to nothing, decoration filling silence.

### Typography
- **Ships:** at most two families with clear roles; display type legible at feed scale (the headline survives thumbnail); spacing set, not defaulted; brand type rules (M-rules) held.
- **Fails:** type as texture (unreadable display lines), scale steps too timid to rank information, more voices than messages, kit fonts swapped for near-enoughs.

### Color & contrast
- **Ships:** palette roles from the brand kit, deployed as the kit intends; text passes the accessibility contrast floor (M-rule) at its real size; the asset holds contrast against the placement's ambient feed color, not against a white artboard.
- **Fails:** off-kit hues introduced by the render, contrast that passes on the artboard and dies in the feed, color used decoratively where it should rank information.

### Composition finish
- **Ships:** clean edges and masks, blends without halos, consistent grain/noise, export honest at the spec'd dims — the mechanical craft that separates made from generated.
- **Fails:** cutout fringing, resolution mismatch between elements, compression artifacts at spec size, visible generation seams (route those to the authenticity rubric's D markers as well).

## Scoring rules
- Band per dimension, then an overall band no higher than the lowest dimension — craft doesn't average.
- Every band assignment cites its observable ("hierarchy: ships — offer, product, CTA in that order at thumbnail; tested at 1:1").
- Every non-ships verdict names its flip condition: the specific change that flips the band.
- Praise cites the observable that earned it; silence is always allowed, flattery never (verdict-integrity R2).
