# Authenticity Rubric — judge dimension #1

The flagship dimension. Audiences reject contrived, cliché, and AI-degenerate work — an ad that trips the fake-detector burns budget AND trust. This rubric makes "reads fake" an evidence claim, not a mood: **every fake-read cites a marker id from the library below, plus where in the asset it appears.** "Feels off" is not a finding. An accept with this dimension unscored is invalid.

Scored against exemplars **both ways**: the taste anchors of real-feeling work (what earning the top band looks like) and of contrived work (what the audience punishes).

## Bands

- **reads-real** — a member of this audience would scroll past it as native content or engage with it as a genuine offer; no marker from the C/K/D families lands; at least one R-marker is cited (praise must be earned — R2 of verdict-integrity).
- **passes-with-notes** — no C/K/D marker at kills-the-asset weight; one or two land at cosmetic weight; the notes name them and their fix.
- **trips-the-detector** — one or more C/K markers land squarely: the audience will clock it as an ad performing at them. Names each marker + location. Not acceptable at the judge gate.
- **slop** — any D-marker lands, or C/K markers stack. The asset damages the brand by existing. Kill.

## Marker library

Durable classes. The current *instances* are perishable and come from recon's per-mission hunts — this library tells the judge what kind of thing to look for; recon tells it what that looks like for this audience right now.

### Contrived — C markers (performing, not being)
- **C1 — stock grammar.** Catalogue lighting, posed delight, the laughing-at-nothing model. The visual register of imagery bought by the sheet, not made for the message.
- **C2 — focus-group voice.** Copy that sells *at* the audience instead of talking *to* them: "Elevate your everyday", benefit-listing in brand-speak, exclamation confidence the product hasn't earned.
- **C3 — fake casual.** Staged spontaneity: tripod-perfect "candid", ring-lit "no filter", UGC framing with agency finish. The audience reads the seams instantly.
- **C4 — assembled tableau.** A cast or scene composed to satisfy a checklist rather than depict a moment that could occur. Plausibility is the test: could this exact scene happen?
- **C5 — borrowed culture.** Slang, memes, or aesthetics owned by a community the brand isn't part of, or used past their shelf. The register mismatch IS the fake signal.

### Cliché — K markers (the median ad)
- **K1 — genre default.** The category's template: gradient wash + bold geometric sans + floating product + badge. Technically fine, invisible by definition.
- **K2 — trend residue.** Last cycle's visual fashion applied because it exists, with no argument from this audience or offer.
- **K3 — first-reach metaphor.** The metaphor anyone would produce in the first minute: rocket for growth, lightbulb for ideas, handshake for trust. If it came instantly, it reads instantly — as filler.

### AI-degenerate — D markers (the render gives itself away)
- **D1 — text garble.** Malformed letterforms, pseudo-words, melted logos anywhere in the render.
- **D2 — anatomy.** Hands, teeth, ears, limb joints, object-grip physics.
- **D3 — plastic life.** Poreless skin, dead eyes, hair merged into background, the airbrushed-mannequin read.
- **D4 — impossible light.** Shadows disagreeing with sources, reflections of absent objects, physics-wrong specularity.
- **D5 — texture uncanny.** Detail density that repeats or swims: fabric weave, foliage, crowds, tiling artifacts.
- **D6 — style soup.** Multiple aesthetics fused without intent — photoreal subject, illustrated ground, 3D props — reading as "the model averaged".

### Real-feeling — R markers (what earns the top band)
- **R1 — specificity.** A real place, a countable thing, an exact shade, a named detail. Specificity is expensive to fake, and audiences know it.
- **R2 — imperfection budget.** Deliberate looseness where this audience expects the human hand — and *only* where they expect it. Uniform polish is a tell; misplaced grit is C3.
- **R3 — native grammar.** Composed like the surface's organic content — its crops, its type conventions, its pacing — not like an ad pasted onto it.
- **R4 — voice match.** Reads like this audience talks when nobody is selling to them. Verified against recon's language evidence, never guessed.
- **R5 — evidence of hand.** Choices only someone with taste would make: an off-center crop that works, a restraint the template wouldn't allow. The opposite of D6.

## Audience-specific fake-detectors

Recon supplies this mission's live detectors: what this segment has praised, what it has ratio'd, which markers are currently hot for this demographic. The judge cites those recon findings alongside library markers — a C5 call, for example, needs recon evidence that the borrowed item is actually owned by someone else in this audience's eyes.

## Citation rules

- Fake-read ⇒ marker id + location in the asset + (for audience-specific calls) the recon tag it stands on.
- reads-real ⇒ at least one R-marker cited with its location. Uncited praise is a verdict-integrity violation.
- Every band assignment names its flip condition: the single change that would move the asset one band.
