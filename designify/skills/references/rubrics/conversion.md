# Conversion Rubric — thumb-stop, glance-speed clarity, CTA, placement fit

Scores whether the creative can do its commercial job: interrupt the scroll, land one message, and make the next step obvious — inside the grammar of its placement. Judged at actual placement size against the placement's real context (feed, grid, story, banner slot), never on a clean artboard.

Prediction discipline: expected performance is stated as **direction + honest band** ("above campaign baseline, modest"), never an invented percentage. These predictions feed the craft file's feedback hook and get graded against Looker actuals — fake precision is graded too.

## Dimensions

### Thumb-stop power
- **Strong:** something in the first glance breaks the placement's ambient pattern — contrast, subject, tension, or specificity the surrounding feed doesn't have. Name WHAT stops the thumb; "eye-catching" is not an observation.
- **Adequate:** visible but polite; earns the glance from users already shopping this category.
- **Weak:** matches the feed's wallpaper. Median-ad camouflage (see craft K1) is the usual cause.

### Message clarity at glance-speed
- **Strong:** a passer-by can say what's offered and for whom after one glance — the 3-second test. One message; everything else subordinated.
- **Adequate:** the message survives a slow read; nothing competes fatally.
- **Weak:** two or more messages fight, or the offer requires the caption to decode. An asset that must be studied has already been scrolled past.

### CTA
- **Strong:** exactly one CTA; a verb; found by the eye at position 2 or 3 of the hierarchy (never buried, never first — the offer earns the click before the ask); contrast-ranked to be seen without shouting.
- **Adequate:** present and findable, but generic ("Learn more" where the offer deserved a sharper verb) or slightly under-ranked.
- **Weak:** absent, plural, or discovered only on inspection.

### Placement fit
- **Strong:** composed for THIS surface: safe zones respected with intent (not just cleared), works under the platform's UI overlays, aspect used rather than endured, text within the placement's rules. Feels native to the surface's grammar (see authenticity R3).
- **Adequate:** compliant but visibly adapted-from-elsewhere — the master asset cropped into the slot.
- **Weak:** overlay collisions, safe-zone violations, text rules broken, or a composition that only worked at the master's aspect. Mechanical violations also fail L1 — this dimension scores the craft above compliance.

### Offer legibility
- **Strong:** the value (price, benefit, or deadline — whichever this campaign leads with) is readable at feed scale without zoom, and consistent with the M-rule claims lines.
- **Adequate:** present but small or subordinated one step too far.
- **Weak:** the offer lives only in the caption, or contradicts an M-rule (that's also a brand-conformance finding — file both).

## Scoring rules
- Band per dimension; overall no higher than the lowest of thumb-stop and message clarity — an ad that neither stops nor lands has no job left.
- Every band cites its observable; every non-strong band names its flip condition.
- Real-deadline urgency is legitimate conversion craft; fabricated urgency is an authenticity C2/C5 finding and an M-rule risk — route it, don't score around it.
