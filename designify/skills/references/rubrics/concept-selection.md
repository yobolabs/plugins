# Concept-Selection Rubric — step-4 scoring

Scores the 2–3 concept cards before the pick. **Hard rule: scores inform the human pick; they never make it.** The judge presents scorecards side by side and stops — no winner declaration, no recommendation, no ranking header. Taste decisions stay human. After the pick, rejected directions get one-line why-nots recorded in the craft file — those why-nots stand and are never rewritten (verdict-integrity R4).

## Dimensions

### 1. Audience fit
Does the direction's causal claim ("why it converts", card field 4) stand on recon's psychology facts for THIS segment?
- **strong** — the claim cites `checked`/`reasoned` recon facts and the mechanism is plausible for this segment specifically.
- **adequate** — grounded but generic: true of this audience, but true of most audiences.
- **weak** — stands on `guessing` tags, on facts recon didn't return, or on no citation at all.

### 2. Authenticity-argument strength
Is the card's authenticity argument (field 5) falsifiable and evidence-backed?
- **strong** — names the markers it avoids and cites recon's hunts (what this audience praised/ratio'd); states what would prove it wrong.
- **adequate** — plausible but uncited; the argument is an assertion recon could probably back.
- **weak** — "it will feel genuine" without markers or evidence — an authenticity argument that can't fail isn't one.

### 3. Distinctiveness vs competitor noise
Judged against recon's comp sweep: would this direction read as ITSELF next to what this audience is already being shown?
- **strong** — visibly not the category default; the difference is purposeful, not novelty for its own sake.
- **adequate** — differentiated in execution but familiar in shape.
- **weak** — the genre-default ad (craft K1) with the brand's palette swapped in.

### 4. Execution risk
Card field 6, challenged: is the biggest risk honestly named, and can the actuators actually deliver this direction?
- **strong** — risk is real and specific with an early sign; the sample prompt sketch (field 3) proves the direction is promptable; trap exposure (which D/C markers this style flirts with) is acknowledged.
- **adequate** — risk named but soft; promptability plausible, unproven.
- **weak** — "might not resonate" non-risks, or a direction the render stack demonstrably struggles with (heavy text-in-image, precise anatomy at scale) with no mitigation.

## Scorecard format

One per concept, in card order, no comparative ranking:

```
Concept <id>: <name>
  audience fit:            <band> — <one-line observable, tags cited>
  authenticity argument:   <band> — <one-line observable, markers/evidence cited>
  distinctiveness:         <band> — <one-line observable vs comp sweep>
  execution risk:          <band> — <one-line observable>
  flip condition: <the single new fact or change that would most move this scorecard>
```

Verdict-integrity applies in full: a low score on any dimension states the concept's steelman first (R1); praise bands cite their evidence (R2); bands are the closed vocabulary — no "interesting", no "has potential" (R3).

## After the pick
Record in the craft file §3: the winner (human-picked) and each rejected direction with its one-line why-not, in the picker's words where given. The why-nots feed calibration — they are data, not drafts.
