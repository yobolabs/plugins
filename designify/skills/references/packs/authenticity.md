# Pack: Authenticity (flagship)

The lens for why work reads REAL or FAKE - the durable theory of the audience's fake-detector, and the markers of work that feels human-made and lived-in.

This is designify's flagship concern (D5): consumers reject contrived, cliche, and AI-degenerate work before they ever evaluate the offer. Authenticity is judge dimension #1; a beautiful asset that trips the fake-detector is a failed asset.

CURRENCY RULE (INV-5): this pack carries the durable THEORY - how fake-detection works and what class-level markers look like. What any specific audience currently mocks, which aesthetics have newly curdled into cliche, and how audiences reacted to comparable campaigns are PERISHABLE - they are recon's authenticity hunts, refreshed per mission, and land in the craft file's authenticity-guardrails section, never here.

## 1. Scope

This lens owns: the fake-detector model (how audiences pattern-match ads against lived reality), the durable markers of real-feeling versus contrived work, audience-class detector profiles, and how gen-AI's default behavior interacts with all of it.

Not covered here: render-defect mechanics (the ai-artifacts trap file is the render-side shadow; this pack explains WHY those defects are fatal), platform-native grammar mechanics (channel-grammar - though native grammar is an authenticity input this pack references), current sentiment and live examples (recon hunts), and brand tone rules (the kit + brand-drift traps).

## 2. Core craft principles

- **The fake-detector is pattern-matching against lived experience.** Every viewer carries a lifetime corpus of real moments, real rooms, real faces, real light. An ad's elements are checked against that corpus below conscious attention. Anything that exists only in advertising - the scenario, the emotion intensity, the casting, the cleanliness, the light - fails the match and registers as OFF before the viewer can say why.
- **The detector fires on accumulation, not single tells.** One slightly staged element is survivable; each additional contrived element (posed smile + spotless kitchen + brochure casting + trailer lighting) multiplies suspicion until the whole asset re-classifies from "moment" to "ad performing a moment". Craft budget: count the tells.
- **Advertising literacy is the floor assumption.** Everyone knows it is an ad. Authenticity is NOT pretending otherwise - disguise is the fastest way to trip the detector. Authenticity is respecting the viewer's intelligence: honest about selling, specific about the human reality of what is sold.
- **Imperfection is evidence - when it is plausible.** Natural available light, off-center framing, real environments with believable clutter, unposed mid-action moments, surface texture (pores, crumbs, wear) all signal unstaged reality. But imperfection PERFORMED (calculated messy bun, artfully placed clutter, fake camera shake) is a double lie and reads worse than honest polish. The imperfections must be the kind reality actually produces.
- **Specificity is the strongest authenticity signal.** Real things are particular: a specific dish with its actual garnish, a room someone plausibly lives in, a person with individual (not averaged) features doing something with a reason. Generic is interchangeable, and the audience correctly reads interchangeable as "nobody real was here". The interchangeability test from conversion-craft (CV-6) is also an authenticity test - the two lenses converge on the same evidence.
- **Emotional plausibility calibrates trust.** The emotion shown must be one the product plausibly produces, at the intensity it plausibly produces it. Quiet satisfaction over a good meal reads true; ecstatic group euphoria over a discount reads as a lie about life. Overclaiming emotion is overclaiming benefit.
- **One plausible world per asset.** Light, styling, props, wardrobe, and grade must all come from the same world. Viewers verify scenes holistically - a subject lit like a studio in a room lit like afternoon reads as assembly, and assembly reads as deception even when every part is individually fine.
- **Native grammar inherits context trust.** Work that looks made-for-the-surface borrows the credibility of that surface's organic content (channel-grammar CG-3). Imported gloss on a casual surface announces "advertisement" louder than any label.
- **Gen-AI defaults to the exact signature of fake.** Generators regress to the averaged, idealized, over-lit, over-smooth center of their training data - which is precisely the stock-genericity the detector is tuned to catch. Left undirected, AI output is contrived BY DEFAULT. The craft is steering hard off the average: specific people, specific places, specific light, controlled imperfection, restrained grade (color-contrast CC-7 is the palette arm of this).
- **Audience-class detector profiles (durable layer; recon refreshes the specifics):**
  - DIGITAL-NATIVE YOUNGER AUDIENCES - the most sensitive class. Hyper-tuned to trying-too-hard: borrowed slang, imported trend grammar, over-production, brand-voice enthusiasm. Reward understatement, self-awareness, creator-grammar work that knows what it is. Punishment is public and social (mockery, not just skipping).
  - FAMILY / VALUE-DRIVEN SHOPPERS - detector tuned to domestic implausibility: showroom kitchens, styled-beyond-eating food, serene mornings that do not exist. Reward recognizable reality: believable portions, believable homes, believable tiredness. Respect their arithmetic - value claims must look like value, not luxury cosplay.
  - PREMIUM BUYERS - detector tuned to cost-cutting tells: cheap stock imagery, over-filtering, effortful luxury signaling. Reward restraint, material honesty, and craft cues (real texture, real making). Loud premium grammar reads as counterfeit premium.
  - PROFESSIONAL / B2B AUDIENCES - detector tuned to corporate theater: handshakes, pointing-at-screens, staged diversity tableaus, triumphant boardrooms. Reward evidence of the actual workflow, plain language, and realism about work. They buy despite ads, via credibility.

## 3. Directive inventory

**AU-1 - Run the fake-detector pass**
- principle: the detector pattern-matches against lived experience - audit every element: does this exist outside advertising?
- preconditions: every asset, at concept AND at render review. The audit walks: scenario (does this moment happen?), casting (do these people exist?), emotion (would it be this strong?), environment (does anyone live/work here?), light (what is the source?).
- failure modes: any cliche-inauthentic trap entry; the audit itself rationalizing ("aspirational is fine") instead of answering the literal question.
- prompt hooks: "candid documentary moment", "unposed", "caught mid-action", "real location, not a studio set".

**AU-2 - Spend a controlled imperfection budget**
- principle: plausible imperfection is evidence of reality - deliberately include the flaws reality produces.
- preconditions: lifestyle and human-subject imagery; food and interiors especially. NOT for marketplace cards (catalog clarity is that surface's honesty) or technical product shots. The imperfections chosen must be the kind that occur naturally: uneven light, honest shadows, a lived-in edge of frame, asymmetry.
- failure modes: performed messiness reading as a second layer of staging; imperfection applied to the PRODUCT itself (the product must be honestly presented, not degraded); AI rendering "imperfection" as defects (the ai-artifacts file owns those).
- prompt hooks: "natural available light", "slightly off-center framing", "lived-in environment", "visible skin texture", "imperfect but appetizing plating", "shot-on-phone realism".

**AU-3 - Individuate everything**
- principle: specificity is the strongest signal - every person, place, and object in frame should be a particular, not an average.
- preconditions: always; the particulars come from recon's brand and audience facts. Where privacy or claims rules prevent naming, individuation moves to visual specificity (a real-looking somewhere, not an anywhere).
- failure modes: averaged AI faces and interchangeable interiors (the gen default); invented specifics recon cannot back - fake specificity is a worse lie than genericity (and can breach claims M-rules); stock-genericity (cliche-stock-energy).
- prompt hooks: "a person with distinctive individual features", "[specific age/build/style from the audience profile]", "a real [cuisine] kitchen with its actual tools", "the specific [dish/product variant] with [its real characteristics]".

**AU-4 - Calibrate the emotion to the stakes**
- principle: emotional plausibility - the feeling shown matches what the product actually produces, at true intensity.
- preconditions: any human expression in frame. The calibration source is recon's audience psychology: what does this moment actually feel like for these people?
- failure modes: ecstatic euphoria over mundane offers (cliche-corporate-smile, cliche-stock-energy); emotion pointed at the camera instead of at the experience; zero affect reading as corporate stillness.
- prompt hooks: "quiet satisfaction", "relaxed genuine expression", "absorbed in the moment, not looking at camera", "subtle natural smile".

**AU-5 - Hold one world**
- principle: one plausible world per asset - light, props, wardrobe, grade all from the same reality.
- preconditions: every composed or generated scene; doubly at batch scale, where the world must also hold ACROSS assets (brand-batch-drift is this directive failing over a series).
- failure modes: composite-look lighting (ai-lighting-mismatch); studio subject in a location scene; props from a richer or poorer world than the setting; grade shifting mid-batch.
- prompt hooks: "consistent natural lighting throughout", "single light source from [the scene's plausible source]", "coherent environment", "everything in the scene belongs to the same place and moment".

**AU-6 - Prompt against the average**
- principle: gen-AI defaults to the fake signature - every prompt must actively steer off the idealized center.
- preconditions: all generated imagery, no exceptions. This directive is the umbrella that AU-2/AU-3/AU-4/AU-5 hooks execute; the per-asset negative/avoid-list (from the traps) is its other half.
- failure modes: undirected prompts yielding the slop aesthetic (cliche-slop-aesthetic); positive hooks present but negated by contradictory style words ("cinematic", "stunning", "epic" pull straight back to the average).
- prompt hooks: prefer grounded photographic language - "documentary photography", "editorial realism", "natural color grade" - and ban the hype adjectives from positive prompts entirely.

**AU-7 - Match the detector profile**
- principle: audience-class profiles differ - design against THIS audience's detector, using recon's refresh of the durable profile.
- preconditions: audience class identified at intake; recon's authenticity hunt returns what this segment currently mocks and trusts (perishable specifics). The pack profile sets the default; recon's findings override it where they conflict.
- failure modes: right asset, wrong audience (premium restraint shown to value shoppers reads as cold; value warmth shown to premium buyers reads as cheap); treating the durable profile as current fact without the recon refresh (INV-5 violation).
- prompt hooks: profile-dependent - the mission's craft file translates the recon findings into this asset's specific hooks and avoid-list.

**AU-8 - Borrow no culture you cannot back**
- principle: borrowed grammar reads as trespass - slang, aesthetics, and subculture signals belong to communities, and audiences police borrowing instantly.
- preconditions: any concept reaching for cultural resonance. The test: does the BRAND (not the designer) have a credible claim to this grammar? If the claim needs explaining, it fails. Current meme/trend specifics are never designed from memory - recon verifies live status, because dead trends are the loudest possible fake signal (INV-5).
- failure modes: youth-pander grammar (cliche-youth-pander); localism claimed without local specifics (cliche-generic-localism); trend references that expired before the campaign shipped.
- prompt hooks: none by default - the safe form of cultural resonance is AU-3's specificity about the audience's actual life, which needs no borrowing.

## 4. Signature questions

1. Would a member of this audience believe this moment happened without a camera crew present? If not, which element gives it away first?
2. Count the tells: how many elements in this frame exist only in advertising? (Budget: approaching zero.)
3. What is SPECIFIC here - and would the interchangeability test (could a competitor run this unchanged?) pass or fail?
4. Is the emotion shown one this product actually produces, at this intensity?
5. Name the light source. Does everything in the frame agree with it, and does the world hold across the whole batch?
6. What did recon's authenticity hunt say this audience mocks - and does anything here rhyme with it?
7. Where is this asset on the polish dial, and is that position chosen for this audience and surface, or inherited from the generator's default?
8. Is any cultural grammar being borrowed, and does the brand have a credible claim to it?

## 5. Placement / asset-class mapping

| Placement class | Weight | What this lens demands here |
|---|---|---|
| Story / full-screen vertical | primary | The most authenticity-sensitive surface: native casual grammar expected; over-production is punished hardest here; AU-2 imperfection budget at its largest |
| Feed image (square / tall portrait) | primary | Middle of the polish dial: polished-but-native; full fake-detector pass; detector-profile match decides the tone |
| Marketplace / product card | primary | Authenticity = honesty of representation: true colors, true scale, true portion; NO imperfection styling - catalog clarity is this surface's truth |
| Hero / landing image | secondary | More production headroom, same one-world rule; must not break the authenticity contract the feed/story assets established |
| Banner / leaderboard (wide) | secondary | Too small for scene-level authenticity; the lens applies to imagery choice (no stock-genericity even at strip size) |

## 6. Exemplar pointers

Slots for the exemplar stories to fill; do not invent files:

- exemplar needed: a real-feeling ad and a contrived ad for the same category, annotated tell by tell against the fake-detector pass (anchor for AU-1/AU-2)
- exemplar needed: a generated portrait pair - default averaged output vs the same brief with anti-average and imperfection hooks (anchor for AU-3/AU-6)
- exemplar needed: one asset per audience-class detector profile, annotated with what that class would mock in the naive version (anchor for AU-7)
- exemplar needed: a one-world consistency pass - a composite-look asset beside its coherent re-render (anchor for AU-5)

The cliche-inauthentic file is this lens's primary failure shadow; ai-artifacts is its render-side shadow (an uncanny defect is an instant detector trip).

- cliche-stock-energy, cliche-overpolish, cliche-diversity-tableau, cliche-corporate-smile (`../traps/cliche-inauthentic.md`) - contrived-scenario tells (violate AU-1, AU-4)
- cliche-slop-aesthetic (`../traps/cliche-inauthentic.md`) - the generator's averaged signature shipped raw (violates AU-6)
- cliche-lifestyle-nobody-lives, cliche-premium-cosplay (`../traps/cliche-inauthentic.md`) - implausible worlds and borrowed grammar (violate AU-4, AU-7)
- cliche-youth-pander, cliche-generic-localism (`../traps/cliche-inauthentic.md`) - unbacked cultural borrowing and fake specificity (violate AU-8, AU-3)
- ai-plastic-skin, ai-face-teeth-eyes, ai-hands-anatomy, ai-hair-merge, ai-accessory-glitch (`../traps/ai-artifacts.md`) - uncanny human rendering, the hardest detector trips (undermine AU-1, AU-2)

- brand-off-tone (`../traps/brand-drift.md`) - a brand performing a personality it does not have is inauthenticity at brand scale (AU-8)
- brand-visual-patchwork (`../traps/brand-drift.md`) - the one-world rule failing across a campaign instead of inside a frame (AU-5 at set scale)
