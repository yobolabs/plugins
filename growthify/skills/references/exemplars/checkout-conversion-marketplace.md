# Exemplar — checkout conversion, e-commerce platform (Fable-direct)

- **mission class:** design-surface · checkout/purchase conversion · e-commerce platform serving small merchants (pre-launch, no measured baseline)
- **lens set:** friction-cro + pricing-economics + incentives-game-theory (hooks-habit, social-viral declared out by metric-family mapping — declaring lenses OUT with the mapping cited is part of the standard)
- **outcome classes:** 10 candidates → 2 kills, 3 dark-pattern exclusions (S2–S3), 8 advanced, top-3 experiment cards; re-runs 0

## Distilled judge standards (what "good" looked like)

1. **Structural diagnosis without numbers.** Pre-launch product = no funnel data; the brief anchored on the category's binding frictions (trust / forced accounts / cost surprise) from the research corpus INSTEAD of inventing a baseline — and explicitly tagged every impact class "precedent-anchored, re-grade at first traffic". Fabricating a diagnosis is the failure this standard guards.
2. **Kills carried steelmen + falsifiers.** Both kills (countdown timers; live-viewer pulses) steelmanned the mechanic's real precedent class first, then killed on platform-generic grounds (can't verify truth per merchant → defaults to fabricated urgency at scale; small-merchant traffic makes honest numbers embarrassing). Each named the observation that would flip it.
3. **Platform-genericity as a fit test.** Every mechanic had to work across ALL merchants without hand-tuning — candidates fine for one storefront but not platform-shippable were graded down on fit, not hand-waved.
4. **Honesty trades made explicit.** The price-transparency card predicted its own metric DROP at one funnel step (earlier sticker shock) and chose a primary metric spanning both steps so the trade nets out — experiment design absorbing the mechanism's known cost.
5. **Network mechanics flagged one-way.** Cross-merchant stored credentials ranked high on impact but ship-and-kill + wargame escalation — compliance/trust surface named, not buried under the upside.
6. **Guardrails from the Goodhart output.** E.g. a trust badge writes a check the platform must cash → claim-rate guardrail; guest checkout risks attribution loss → payment-fingerprint repeat-purchase guardrail with frozen definition.
