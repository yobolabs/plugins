## Residual frontier-only behaviors (observed this diff, generalized)

One mission — these are observations, not yet recurrence-proven. Items marked with their class; where a heuristic is teachable but the spontaneous noticing is not, both are said.

1. **Structural-thesis-first framing** — composing scattered diagnostic components into one organizing inversion and making the portfolio's top ranks trace to it. The framework held all three components of the thesis as separate tagged observations and shipped a parts list; Fable shipped the machine. *Procedure* (add the compose-then-rank step) with a *capability* residue on composition quality — the step can force a sentence to exist; it cannot force it to be the right inversion.
2. **Single-dominant-lever taste** — distinguishing the mechanic that changes the system's steady state (a recurring trigger installed at object birth) from the one that improves a session (a next-best-action runway), ranking the former first even at lower evidence grade, and organizing everything downstream of it. *Capability.* Partially floor-raised by the default-wiring pass and the structural-precondition ranking rule (see patch list), but the unprompted noticing is the frontier part — the growth analog of the wargame doc's discriminator taste.
3. **Already-built-sweep reframing** — treating the recon inventory as raw material, not coverage. "Channel deploy exists" hid the delivery-location lever; "reminders exist" hid the wire-it-at-birth default. The failure mechanism is the "exists" gloss. *Procedure* (sweep must ask, per substrate: what does this NOT do by default, and what mechanic would make it the default?).
4. **Cross-card dependency honesty** — a kill criterion in one experiment card pointing at another card ("most digests empty → fix #1 first"). The framework threaded one dependency (enabler→E-1) but not systematically. *Procedure* (dependency field in the experiment-card template + empty-content guard wherever a card's content is produced by another card).
5. **Delivery-location check** — "does the product's recurring value arrive where the user already lives, or does it demand a visit?" The single question behind the ground-truth arm's top three mechanics. *Knowledge* (hooks-habit lens pack entry).
6. **Seam-level consumer-experience specificity** — one clause that carries a known loop-killer: "the teammate lands on the artifact, not a signup wall." *Knowledge* (social-viral pack: invite-landing rule) with a small taste residue in knowing which clause matters.
7. **Portfolio tightness under no quota** — 10 on-mission candidates doing the work of 24, with the weakest lens dropped and the reason stated. The framework's depth bound behaved as a quota and manufactured padding (and one off-metric-family advance its own judge had killed the sibling of). *Procedure* (bound is a ceiling; off-target = kill regardless of lens origin; scoped-sliver option preserved — the scoped pricing sliver earned a genuine card the ground-truth arm missed).
8. **Precondition-gated kill resolution** — killing the naive form of a mechanic now with an observable flip condition, rather than advancing the sophisticated form before its precondition is observed (login-streak killed, review-streak named as the flip vs the framework advancing the review-streak unconditionally at low rank). *Knowledge* (kill-craft note for the judge prompt) — minor.

Patch targets from this calibration (enumerated with rulings in the mission's calibration record): SKILL.md — thesis-compose step, default-wiring pass, ceiling-not-quota; growth-judge prompt — off-target consistency; experiment-card template — dependency field; hooks-habit pack — delivery-location; social-viral pack — invite-landing, population-size precondition; ranking.md — structural-precondition floats above evidence-grade lexicography; dark-patterns.md — borderline line for real-but-business-manufactured expiry (the one substrate where the arms reached opposite defensible verdicts). **All 10 forward-gap patches APPLIED 2026-07-07 across 7 files; validator PASS.** The ≈70% row above measured the PRE-patch run — a re-calibration on a fresh mission is pending to confirm the projected low-to-mid-80s lift; treat the projection as unmeasured until then.

## When to spend frontier-model budget on a growth mission

- **Frame-risk missions** — when the right deliverable might be a reframe of where/how value is delivered rather than a mechanics list. This calibration's entire gap lived there. If the mission smells like "the product's loop itself is miswired," that is frontier terrain.
- **First mission on a new surface** — the structural thesis compounds into every subsequent mission on that surface. Once a thesis exists (in the mission dir or generalized into `insights/`), feed it to the framework as recon input — the cheapest fix of all: the framework demonstrably executes well UNDER a thesis; it failed only at originating one.
- **Single-big-bet portfolios** — when the team will ship one mechanic, not pick from a menu, single-dominant-lever taste is the whole game. When the team wants an A/B menu with guardrails, the framework is already at parity or better.
- **The framework is the right spend for**: breadth coverage (it out-covered Fable on the lapsed cohort), resurrection/win-back sweeps, guardrail/Goodhart engineering, kill breadth as decision assets, integrity-critical portfolios (its ledger/flag mechanics caught the ground-truth arm's own defects), and re-runs on a surface with a known thesis.
- **Judge duty on calibrations** stays frontier — and note the identity caveat: when ground truth and judge are the same model tier, style-agreement bias is live; ground every ruling in a card.
