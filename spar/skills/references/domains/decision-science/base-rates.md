# Decision Science — Base Rates

Outside-view numbers. Any plan violating these without explanation is a guaranteed red-team reaction.

## Planning fallacy magnitude
Students predicted thesis completion in 33.9 days; actual mean 55.5 days (+64%); fewer than 30% finished by their own predicted date. Pattern replicates across domains: predictions track best-case, not modal case.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Buehler, Griffin & Ross, "Exploring the Planning Fallacy," JPSP 1994 · grade: A

## Megaproject cost overruns
9 of 10 transport megaprojects overrun budget. Average overrun: rail +45%, bridges/tunnels +34%, roads +20% (constant prices, 258 projects, 20 nations, 70 years). Overruns have not improved over time.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Flyvbjerg, Holm & Buhl, JAPA 2002 · grade: A

## IT project overruns — fat tails
Average IT project cost overrun +27%, but 1 in 6 is a "black swan": +200% cost, +70% schedule (1,471 projects). The tail, not the mean, is the risk.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Flyvbjerg & Budzier, "Why Your IT Project May Be Riskier Than You Think," HBR 2011 · grade: A

## Software project outcomes
31% successful (on time/budget/scope), 50% challenged, 19% failed. Small projects ~90% success; large projects <10%. Success rate has hovered ~30% across three decades of reports.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Standish Group CHAOS Report 2020 · grade: B (proprietary methodology, criticized definitions — use directionally)

## Overconfidence in interval estimates
90–98% confidence intervals capture the true value roughly 40–60% of the time. Managers and experts show the same miss rates as students; calibration training helps, expertise alone does not.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Alpert & Raiffa 1982 (in Kahneman/Slovic/Tversky, Judgment Under Uncertainty); Russo & Schoemaker, Decision Traps, 1989 · grade: A

## Expert forecasting accuracy
82,361 forecasts from 284 experts: long-range political/economic predictions barely beat chance; fame negatively correlated with accuracy. Trained superforecasters beat intelligence analysts (with classified access) by ~30% on the same questions.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Tetlock, Expert Political Judgment, 2005; Mellers et al., Psych Science 2014 · grade: A

## Organizational decision failure rate
~Half of strategic organizational decisions fail (not used, not sustained, or abandoned); ~70% of the 400 decisions studied considered only a single alternative; failure rates roughly double when decisions are imposed by edict vs. built with participation.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Nutt, Why Decisions Fail, 2002 · grade: B

## Process beats analysis 6:1
Across 1,048 investment decisions, decision process quality explained ~6x more of the variance in outcomes (ROI/growth vs expectations) than depth of analysis.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Lovallo & Sibony, "The Case for Behavioral Strategy," McKinsey Quarterly 2010 · grade: B (consultancy self-report survey; large N)

## Sunk cost — behavioral magnitude
Theatergoers randomly given full-price season tickets attended significantly more shows than those given discounts — having paid more drove consumption of an identical good. Sunk cost responds to amount paid, not value received.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Arkes & Blumer, OBHDP 1985 (field experiment) · grade: A

## Base-rate neglect default
Given individuating description + base rate, subjects judge by resemblance and largely ignore the base rate (lawyer/engineer paradigm) — unless the base rate is causally framed. Assume any vivid narrative in a decision memo is displacing the reference class.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Kahneman & Tversky, "On the Psychology of Prediction," Psych Review 1973 · grade: A
