# Decision Science — Question Battery

Run BEFORE attacking. Each question targets an unknown-unknown a smart generalist skips.

## Reference class
"What's the reference class, what's its base rate, and if you claim exemption — exempt on what evidence?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Kahneman & Lovallo 1993; Flyvbjerg RCF · grade: A

## Door type
"Is this a one-way or two-way door? What would you pay to make it reversible, and is that option actually available?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Bezos 2015 shareholder letter · grade: A

## The opposite case
"What would have to be true for the opposite choice to be the right one? Can you state the case against, well enough that its holder would sign it?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Lafley & Martin, Playing to Win, 2013 ("what would have to be true"); Caplan's ideological Turing test, 2011 · grade: B

## Kill criteria
"What observable result, by what date, kills this? Decided now — not after the data arrives."
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Staw 1976 escalation literature; Duke, Quit, 2022 · grade: B

## Pre-mortem
"It's 12 months later and this failed. What killed it? Give three distinct causes, not one."
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Klein, HBR 2007 · grade: A

## Opportunity cost
"What does this crowd out? If this team/capital were freed tomorrow, what's the best alternative use — and why is this better than that?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Nutt 2002 (single-option decisions fail); Heath & Heath, Decisive, 2013 · grade: B

## Ruin check
"Can you take this bet 100 times? What's the loss that ends the game, and what's its probability under the WRONG model, not yours?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Kelly 1956; Lowenstein, When Genius Failed, 2000 (LTCM) · grade: B

## Disconfirming evidence
"What information would change your mind — and what did you actually do to obtain it? A test that can't fail is not a test."
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Wason 1960; Popper, falsifiability · grade: A

## Independent estimates
"Were estimates collected independently before anyone spoke, or did the room converge on the first number said aloud?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Kahneman, Sibony & Sunstein, Noise, 2021; Englich et al. 2006 (anchoring in experts) · grade: A

## Deadline reality
"Is the deadline real or self-imposed? What concretely happens if you decide two weeks later — and is that cost larger than the value of the information you'd gain?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: optionality/real-options framing — Dixit & Pindyck, Investment Under Uncertainty, 1994 · grade: B

## New-owner test
"If you were hired today with no history here, would you start this? If no — what, other than sunk cost, keeps it alive?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Grove, Only the Paranoid Survive, 1996 (Intel memory exit, 1985) · grade: B

## Whose forecast
"Whose numbers are these, and what's that person's track record and incentive? A forecast is testimony, not data."
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Tetlock 2005 (expertise ≠ accuracy); Flyvbjerg 2002 (strategic misrepresentation in project forecasts) · grade: A
