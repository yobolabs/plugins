# Business Strategy — Question Battery

Expert questions for any strategy spar. Run at intake.

## The Power question
"When this works, what stops it being copied? Name the specific Power (scale, network, counter-positioning, switching costs, brand, cornered resource, process) and when it arrives — pre-Power traction is a bounty, not a moat."
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Helmer, 7 Powers, 2016 · grade: B

## Move two
"What does the incumbent do in month three after noticing you — price, bundle, lock up the channel, litigate, acquire your supplier? Which responses are profitable for them, and does your model survive those?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: counter-positioning analysis, Helmer 2016; game-theoretic strategy canon (Ghemawat, Commitment, 1991) · grade: B

## Bottom-up TAM
"Name the first 100 customers. Multiply identifiable buyers × achievable price × channel-feasible share. Does your bottom-up number reconcile with what incumbents actually earn in this niche?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: standard VC diligence; Gurley, Above the Crowd, 2014 · grade: B

## The job
"What job is the customer hiring this for, what do they fire to hire you, and what's the switching cost of the firing? Include non-consumption as the competitor."
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Christensen et al., HBR 2016 · grade: A

## Why now
"What changed — technology, regulation, behavior — that makes this work today when it didn't five years ago? If nothing: who tried it, and where are the bodies?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Sequoia pitch-deck template; VC practice canon · grade: B

## Channel math at maturity
"What's CAC in the named channel at month 24, after early adopters are exhausted and competitors bid the channel up? What's the plan when this channel saturates?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: growth-practice canon; corroborated by CB Insights unit-economics failure share, 2025 · grade: B

## The reject list
"What is this strategy explicitly NOT doing? If nothing was rejected, no choice was made."
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Porter, "What Is Strategy?", HBR 1996; Rumelt 2011 · grade: A

## Profit-pool position
"Where in the value chain does the profit actually pool, and are you capturing it or feeding someone who does? Who has pricing power over YOU (suppliers, platforms, key hires)?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Gadiesh & Gilbert, "Profit Pools," HBR 1998; Porter five forces, HBR 1979/2008 · grade: A

## Platform kill-switch
"Which single partner, platform, or customer can materially damage you with one policy change? What's the dependency cap and the hedge?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Zynga 2012 10-K risk factors (the canonical disclosure); platform-economics literature (Cusumano/Gawer) · grade: B

## Cheapest kill test
"What's the cheapest, fastest experiment that would kill this strategy if it's wrong? Why isn't it already running?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: lean-startup practice (Ries 2011), sharpened by pre-mortem discipline (Klein, HBR 2007) · grade: B

## Base-rate escape
"Pioneer failure is 47%; most M&A destroys value; ~65% of VC deals lose money. Which base rate applies to this plan, and what's the specific mechanism — not team quality — by which you escape it?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Golder & Tellis 1993; Christensen HBR 2011; Correlation Ventures · grade: A

## Copycat stress test
"A competent, funded team gets your full plan tomorrow. What do you still have that they can't take — and if the answer is 'head start,' how long is it really?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Helmer, 7 Powers, 2016 (Power vs. operational excellence distinction) · grade: B

## Revenue-ranked incumbent sweep (recon standing rule)
"Name the LARGEST company already monetizing this exact buyer — by revenue/users, not the most similar-looking startup." Run at recon, always: a GoHighLevel-class miss (the giant hiding in the very listicle you cited) is disqualifying — rerun the sweep.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: spar framework calibration 2026-07 (GHL miss), references/capability-gap.md · grade: B

## Deflation
"Is the value you sell deflating — AI/commoditization shrinking it quarterly? Which half of the value prop deflates slower — sell that half."
date: 2026-07-06 · source: spar framework calibration 2026-07 (codegen-deflation attack), references/capability-gap.md · grade: B

## Null hypothesis
"What is the company already doing that this strategy competes with for marginal hours? The default motion enters the ranking as the null hypothesis — every other motion must state why a marginal hour beats it."
date: 2026-07-06 · source: spar framework calibration 2026-07, references/capability-gap.md · grade: B

## Evidence velocity
"What is the fastest instrument producing an unambiguous verdict on the core unknown — cost in days and dollars? Payment-grade evidence preferred: payment > usage > signup > attention."
date: 2026-07-06 · source: spar framework calibration 2026-07, references/capability-gap.md · grade: B
