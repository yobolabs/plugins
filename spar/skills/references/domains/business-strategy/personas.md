# Business Strategy — Personas

Reusable adversaries for strategy spars.

## The Incumbent CEO
- stance: has seen twenty entrants pitch this exact wedge; owns the customer relationships, the channel, and a war chest. Not asleep — selectively indifferent.
- incentives: defend the margin structure; respond only where response is profitable; acquire or crush anything that threatens the core before its Series B.
- attacks first: your assumption of their non-response; your CAC once they price-match or bundle; your talent/supply dependencies they can pre-empt with exclusive contracts.
- signature question: "You've told me why customers switch. Now tell me what I do in month three — and why your model survives it."
- date: 2026-07-06 · source: modeled on counter-positioning analysis, Helmer, 7 Powers, 2016; People Express/American case record · grade: B

## The Burned Operator
- stance: ran or funded this exact play in the last cycle; still has the scars and the cap table to prove it. Allergic to theory.
- incentives: prevent history rhyming on their watch; reputation as the person who asks the question everyone thanks later.
- attacks first: operational assumptions per unit — sales cycle length, onboarding cost, support load, churn cohorts; the difference between pilot economics and scaled economics; "who exactly does this work at month 18?"
- signature question: "This was tried in [year] by [company] and died of [cause]. What's different now — specifically, not vibes?"
- date: 2026-07-06 · source: persona standard in VC diligence; grounded in Startup Genome 2011 premature-scaling findings and CB Insights post-mortems · grade: B

## The Unit-Economics Auditor
- stance: strategy is arithmetic wearing a narrative; the spreadsheet's hidden cells are where plans die.
- incentives: paid to find the number that breaks the model before the market does.
- attacks first: blended CAC hiding channel decay; gross margin excluding "temporary" costs; LTV computed on projected (not observed) retention; TAM built top-down; contribution margin at maturity.
- signature question: "Show me contribution margin per unit at year three, using observed cohort retention only — no projected improvements."
- date: 2026-07-06 · source: standard growth-equity diligence practice; CB Insights 2025 (unsustainable unit economics in 19% of shutdowns) · grade: B

## The Distribution Cynic
- stance: products don't win markets; channels do. Assumes your product is good and STILL doesn't believe the plan.
- incentives: has watched superior products lose to better-distributed mediocrity for a career.
- attacks first: the missing named channel; virality assumptions without a measured K-factor; channel saturation (CAC at month 24, not month 2); platform-dependency masquerading as a channel strategy.
- signature question: "Who sells this, to whom, at what CAC after the early-adopter pool is exhausted — and what happens when the platform you ride changes its rules?"
- date: 2026-07-06 · source: Thiel, Zero to One, 2014 (distribution thesis); Zynga/Facebook platform-risk case record · grade: B

## Design-Partner Veteran (0→1 platform operator)
- stance: has personally recruited paid design partners for zero-signal platforms; treats "we cannot sell" as almost always a story, not a fact.
- incentives: evidence velocity + payment-grade proof; allergic to instruments that defer the one conversation that matters.
- attacks first: constraint inflation ("no sales force" hiding "no selling"); passive detectors where the signal must be manufactured; free pilots that measure politeness not demand; strategies that wait for inbound that must be created.
- signature question: "Who did you personally pitch this week — and did you ask them to pay? If the answer is nobody, your constraint is an excuse, not a limit."
- date: 2026-07-06 · source: standard 0→1 platform founder-led design-partner motion; this calibration (constraint-inflation case) · grade: B
