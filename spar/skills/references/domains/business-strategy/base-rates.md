# Business Strategy — Base Rates

The numbers that call bullshit on strategy decks.

## Startup survival (all businesses, US)
~20% of new employer establishments fail within year 1; ~50% by year 5; ~65% by year 10. Stable across decades and cycles; varies by industry (year-1 failure: agriculture ~7% to mining ~31%).
date: 2026-07-06 · source: BLS Business Employment Dynamics, establishment-age tables (bls.gov/bdm/bdmage.htm), cohorts through 2024 · grade: A

## VC-backed outcome distribution
~65% of VC financings return <1x; <4% of invested capital produces ≥10x. Returns are power-law, not normal — the median VC-backed startup loses money for investors.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Correlation Ventures proprietary dataset (Coats, "We're Still Not Normal," 2019; Forbes/Booth 2013 coverage) · grade: B (proprietary but the standard reference)

## Why startups die
Of 431 VC-backed shutdowns since 2023: ran out of capital 70% (proximate), poor product-market fit 43%, bad timing 29%, unsustainable unit economics 19% (multi-cause; sums >100%). Earlier CB Insights rounds: "no market need" 35–42%.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: CB Insights startup-failure post-mortem series, 2021 + 2025 editions (cbinsights.com/research/report/startup-failure-reasons-top) · grade: B

## M&A value destruction
70–90% of acquisitions destroy value for the acquirer (HBR/Christensen 2011, reaffirmed 2024 retrospective). KPMG (1999): 83% failed to boost shareholder returns. Bain 2025: only ~30% of strategic deals met internal targets. BCG 2026: 60% trailed acquirer's pre-announcement share price at 12 months. Definitions differ; the direction never does.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Christensen et al., HBR March 2011; KPMG 1999 "Unlocking Shareholder Value"; Bain M&A Report 2025; BCG M&A Report 2026 · grade: A (convergent multi-study)

## First-mover reality
Market pioneers: 47% failure rate, ~10% mean market share among survivors. Early followers (enter avg. 13 years later): 8% failure, ~28% share, and tend to gain share. "First-mover advantage" is mostly survivorship narrative.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Golder & Tellis, "Pioneer Advantage: Marketing Logic or Marketing Legend?", J. Marketing Research 1993 (500 brands, 50 categories) · grade: A

## New product failure rate
~40% of new products fail at launch (varies by category ~35–49%). The oft-quoted "80–90% fail" is a myth with no primary source — knowing this cuts both ways in an argument.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Castellion & Markham, "Perspective: New Product Failure Rates," JPIM 2013 · grade: A

## Corporate longevity compression
Average S&P 500 tenure: 30–35 years (late 1970s) → forecast 15–20 years during the 2020s. Roughly half the index turns over per ~decade. Incumbency is a melting asset; "we'll partner with/get acquired by an incumbent" plans should price incumbent churn.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Innosight Corporate Longevity Forecast 2021 (innosight.com/insight/creative-destruction) · grade: B

## Corporate transformation success
~30% of large-scale change/transformation programs achieve their goals (McKinsey's long-standing "70% fail" survey finding; contested methodology, directionally corroborated across consultancies). Use as a prior on any "we'll transform the org" pillar.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: McKinsey transformation surveys (2015–2021); critique: Hughes, J. Change Management 2011 · grade: C (survey-based, definition-sensitive — flagged accordingly)

## Premature scaling prevalence
~70% of startups in the Startup Genome sample scaled some dimension (team, marketing, product) ahead of validated demand; premature scalers show materially worse outcomes and stall at lower valuations.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Startup Genome Report Extra: Premature Scaling, 2011 (3,200+ startups, self-reported) · grade: B

## Diversification discount
Diversified conglomerates trade at a ~13–15% discount to the imputed value of their parts (US data). "Synergy across unrelated units" claims fight this prior.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Berger & Ofek, "Diversification's Effect on Firm Value," J. Financial Economics 1995 · grade: A

## Pivot prevalence among winners
No reliable base rate exists for "successful startups that pivoted" — widely cited figures (e.g., "most unicorns pivoted") trace to anecdote collections, not systematic samples. Treat pivot statistics in decks as unsourced until shown otherwise.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: absence-of-evidence note after search of academic + industry literature, 2026-07-06 · grade: C (deliberate null entry)

## PLG free→paid conversion (2026)
~9% median free→paid across 2026 PLG benchmarks. A plan modeling materially above the median needs a named mechanism. (The `gtm` pack carries the banded free-to-paid entry by motion type — load it alongside.)
date: 2026-07-06 · source: 2026 PLG benchmarks via spar framework calibration fact base, references/capability-gap.md · grade: B

## Time to $1M ARR
~2.75 years median for SaaS startups reaching $1M ARR. Assuming materially faster is planning to be top-decile — fine as ambition, dishonest as a base case.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: 2026 SaaS benchmarks via spar framework calibration fact base, references/capability-gap.md · grade: B

## Agency-tool price points
AI/automation platforms sold to agencies and SMB operators cluster at $300–1,500/mo (GoHighLevel-class chassis pricing). Price a competing motion against this band, not against enterprise ACVs.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: 2026 agency-platform public pricing via spar framework calibration, references/capability-gap.md · grade: B

## Founder-led design-partner sales = the standard 0→1 platform motion
The default first motion for a 0→1 platform: founder-led sales to 3–5 paid design partners. Founder-led selling to N≤5 is not a sales force; a plan that forbids it under a no-sales-force constraint has inflated the constraint.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: 0→1 platform practitioner canon; spar framework calibration 2026-07, references/capability-gap.md · grade: B
