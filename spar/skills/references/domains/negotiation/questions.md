# Negotiation — Question Battery

Expert questions a smart generalist skips. Run at intake of any deal spar.

## BATNA, priced
"What exactly happens if there's no deal — priced in numbers, with dates? And what's THEIR no-deal world worth?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Fisher & Ury 1981 · grade: A

## Reservation price, written
"What's the worst deal you'll sign, written down before first contact — and who off-table holds you to it?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Raiffa, The Art and Science of Negotiation, 1982 · grade: A

## Their ratification story
"What does your counterpart have to say to their boss/board/spouse to get this approved? Draft that sentence for them."
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Malhotra & Bazerman, Negotiation Genius, 2007 · grade: B

## Issue inventory
"List every issue in play beyond price — timing, scope, risk allocation, exclusivity, references, renewal. What's cheap for you and expensive for them?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: integrative-bargaining literature; Bazerman & Neale, Negotiating Rationally, 1992 · grade: A

## Compatible-issue check
"Is there an issue where you both want the SAME thing and neither has said so? (~20% of negotiators lose value exactly here.)"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Thompson & Hrebec, Psych Bulletin 1996 · grade: A

## Authority check
"Can the person across the table sign? If not, what's the ratification path — and are your concessions being pocketed while theirs stay provisional?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Schelling, The Strategy of Conflict, 1960 · grade: B

## Their clock
"What pressure is on THEIR side — quarter end, quota, expiring mandate, inventory, financing window? What do you know about their deadline that they haven't told you?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Moore, JESP 2004 (deadlines); standard deal practice · grade: B

## Winner's curse check
"If they accepted your offer instantly, what would you conclude? What does eagerness on their side tell you about the asset?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Bazerman & Samuelson, JCR 1983 · grade: A

## First-offer plan
"Who anchors, at what number, justified by what? If they anchor first, what's your scripted re-anchor?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Galinsky & Mussweiler, JPSP 2001; Orr & Guthrie 2005 · grade: A

## Concession choreography
"What does your pattern of concessions signal? (Shrinking increments read as approaching a wall; accelerating ones as desperation.) What's the planned sequence?"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Raiffa 1982; concession-pattern research summarized in Thompson, The Mind and Heart of the Negotiator · grade: B

## Post-signature reality
"Who performs this deal after signing, who monitors, and what's the renegotiation path when circumstances change? A deal the other side resents is a deal you'll negotiate twice."
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Curhan et al. 2006 (subjective value → future behavior) · grade: B

## Deadline forensics
"For every deadline in this deal: who set it, what physically changes when it passes, and has anyone tested it? (Conditional acceptance — the Farpoint Gambit — is the test.)"
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Robinson, Negotiation Journal 1995 · grade: A
