# Negotiation — Base Rates

Effect sizes and frequencies for calling bluffs on deal plans.

## First-offer anchoring effect size
Meta-analytic correlation between first offer and final outcome: r = .497 — the single best predictor of where a deal lands. Professional experience reduces but does not eliminate it.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Orr & Guthrie, "Anchoring, Information, Expertise, and Negotiation," Ohio St. J. Disp. Resol. 2005 · grade: A

## Who-moves-first advantage
Negotiators who made the first offer captured more value across buyer/seller roles; effect driven by anchoring on the opponent's counteroffer generation. Caveat (later work): first offers hurt when the other side has private information you lack.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Galinsky & Mussweiler, JPSP 2001; Loschelder/Schaerer information-asymmetry follow-ons, 2014–2016 · grade: A

## Precision effect magnitude
Precise first offers (e.g., $4,985) received counteroffers conceding materially less than round offers ($5,000); field-replicated on real-estate listings. Reverses against experts when precision lacks justification.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Mason et al., JESP 2013; Loschelder et al., Psych Science 2016 · grade: A

## Lose-lose frequency
Across 32 studies / 5,000+ participants, ~20% of negotiators agreed to outcomes BOTH sides valued less than an available alternative on compatible issues — pure failure to ask.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Thompson & Hrebec, Psych Bulletin 1996 · grade: A

## Ultimatum rejection threshold
Offers below ~20% of the pie are rejected roughly half the time, across dozens of countries and stake sizes — people pay real money to punish perceived unfairness. Fairness perception is a hard constraint, not decoration.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Güth et al. 1982 (original); Camerer, Behavioral Game Theory, 2003 (cross-cultural survey) · grade: A

## Salary negotiation propensity + payoff (historical → current)
Carnegie Mellon masters graduates (2003 data): 57% of men vs 7% of women negotiated the first offer; negotiating gained ~7.4% (~$4,000). Recent studies flip propensity (54% of women vs 44% of men attempted negotiation, 2023 MBA data) but pay gaps persist — treat "who asks" as era-dependent, "asking pays" as durable.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Babcock & Laschever, Women Don't Ask, 2003; Elsesser, Forbes 2023 summarizing recent MBA-graduate research · grade: B (mixed-era evidence)

## Deadline revelation
Negotiators with final deadlines did better revealing them than concealing them — counterparties compressed concessions into the real window. People predict the opposite; the intuition is reliably wrong.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Moore, JESP 2004 · grade: A

## First-five-minutes predictiveness
Conversational dynamics (activity, mirroring, prosody) in the first 5 minutes predicted ~30% of variance in negotiation outcomes.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Curhan & Pentland, "Thin Slices of Negotiation," JAP 2007 · grade: A

## Agent-principal gap, quantified
Real-estate agents selling their own homes: +3.7% sale price, +9.5 days on market vs. client listings — the size of the "good enough to close" discount your agent applies to you.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Levitt & Syverson, RES 2008 · grade: A

## Anchoring survives expertise
Experienced judges given a randomly generated (dice-roll) sentencing demand anchored on it — high anchor ~8 months vs low anchor ~5 months for the identical case. Do not assume professional counterparties are immune, or that you are.
date: 2026-07-06 · source: Englich, Mussweiler & Strack, PSPB 2006 · grade: A
