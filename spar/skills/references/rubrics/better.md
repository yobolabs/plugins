# The Better Rubric

"Better" never means "more complex." Complexity is a **cost** to be justified on another axis, explicitly — never an achievement. A solution, plan, or opportunity improves only along these axes:

| Axis | Question | Signals |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Elegant** | Does the shape of the solution match the shape of the problem? | One clause settles three disputes; the fix removes steps instead of adding them; special cases collapse into the general case |
| **Simple** | Fewest moving parts that fully solve it? | Fewer approvals, fewer dependencies, fewer parties who must cooperate; a newcomer predicts the outcome correctly |
| **Performant** | Faster / cheaper on the path that matters? | Measured on the hot path the mission names — time-to-close, cost per acquisition, hours per week — not micro-optimized everywhere |
| **Usable** | Easier for its consumer (a person, a team, a counterparty) to act on correctly and hard to misuse? | Right defaults; the obvious next step is the correct one; errors and rejections say what to do instead |
| **Integrable** | Easier to plug into what already exists — fewer assumptions imposed on its environment? | Fits existing processes, relationships, and contracts; narrow interface; no bespoke ritual demanded of others |
| **Understandable** | Can someone who wasn't in the room explain why it works? | The rationale survives retelling; observable outcomes; names and terms that carry the model |

## Scoring

- Score only the axes a change **moves** (±), don't ritually grade all six.
- Every plus must name its cost axis if one exists ("more performant, less simple: adds an intermediary — justified because time-to-close is the primary axis").
- **Ties break toward the mission brief's primary axis.**
- Consumer count and strategic anchor beat speculative breadth: "extract the playbook when the second consumer materializes" is a valid — often the right — top ranking.
- Anti-pattern to reject on sight: an "improvement" whose only movement is negative on simple/understandable while claiming unmeasured gains or hypothetical flexibility.
